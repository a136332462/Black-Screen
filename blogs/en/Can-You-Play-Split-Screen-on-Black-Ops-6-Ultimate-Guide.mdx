## Understanding Split-Screen in Call of Duty: Black Ops 6

Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 does support split-screen gameplay, but with significant platform and mode restrictions that many players find frustrating. Understanding these limitations is crucial before attempting to set up local co-op sessions.

### Platform Availability

Split-screen in Black Ops 6 is exclusively available on:
- **PlayStation 5**: Full split-screen support
- **Xbox Series X|S**: Full split-screen support
- **PC**: Not supported
- **PlayStation 4**: Not supported  
- **Xbox One**: Not supported

This platform restriction means that only next-generation console owners can enjoy local multiplayer experiences. PC gamers and previous-generation console owners must rely on online multiplayer instead.

### Supported Game Modes

Split-screen functionality works only in specific game modes:
- **Multiplayer Mode**: Two players can split the screen in online and offline multiplayer matches
- **Zombies Mode**: Split-screen is available for cooperative zombie survival gameplay
- **Campaign Mode**: Split-screen is not supported—only single-player gameplay available

These restrictions significantly limit local co-op options compared to earlier Call of Duty titles that offered more flexible split-screen support.

## Step-by-Step Split-Screen Setup Guide

Setting up split-screen in Black Ops 6 requires careful attention to controller configuration and account management. Follow these detailed steps for a smooth setup process.

### Initial Setup Requirements

Before starting, ensure you have:
1. A PlayStation 5 or Xbox Series X|S console
2. Two compatible controllers (both must be properly charged and connected)
3. Two separate user profiles on your console
4. Two Activision accounts (or one account with a guest profile option)
5. An active internet connection (required even for local play)

### Setup Process for PlayStation 5

The PlayStation 5 setup process involves several specific steps:

1. **Launch Black Ops 6** and navigate to the main menu
2. Select either **Multiplayer** or **Zombies** mode
3. Connect your second controller and press the **X button**
4. When prompted, sign in with a second PlayStation account (or create a guest profile)
5. Link the second account to an Activision account if required
6. Both players should now appear on the screen
7. Select your desired game mode and start playing

The second player must have their own PlayStation Network account, though guest profiles may work in some scenarios.

### Setup Process for Xbox Series X|S

Xbox setup follows a similar pattern with Xbox-specific controls:

1. Start Black Ops 6 and access the main menu
2. Choose **Multiplayer** or **Zombies** mode
3. Connect your second controller and press the **A button**
4. Sign in with a second Xbox profile (ensure it's linked to an Activision account)
5. Wait for both player profiles to appear on screen
6. Launch your selected game mode

Xbox Live Gold or Game Pass Ultimate subscriptions may be required for both accounts depending on your region and subscription tier.

## Common Split-Screen Issues and Solutions

Split-screen gaming often encounters technical difficulties that can disrupt your gaming experience. Understanding these problems helps resolve them quickly.

### Screen Resolution and Display Problems

Split-screen divides your screen, which can cause:
- Reduced visual clarity on smaller displays
- Difficulty identifying distant enemies
- Interface elements appearing cramped or overlapping

**Solution**: Use larger displays (55 inches or above) for optimal split-screen visibility. Adjust in-game HUD scaling if available to improve readability.

### Performance and Frame Rate Issues

Rendering two perspectives simultaneously can impact performance:
- Frame rate drops during intense combat sequences
- Texture quality reduction in split-screen mode
- Input lag affecting responsiveness

**Solution**: Close background applications, ensure proper console ventilation, and consider playing on lower graphic settings if performance issues persist.

### Controller Connection Problems

Second controller disconnections are frequent issues:
- Controller not being recognized by the game
- Inputs registering to the wrong player
- Controller battery issues causing dropouts

**Solution**: Reset controllers, update controller firmware, and ensure both controllers are fully charged before starting a session.
