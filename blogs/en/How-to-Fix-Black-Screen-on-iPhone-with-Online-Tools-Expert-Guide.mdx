### What “iPhone Black Screen” Means
An iPhone can show a black screen when iOS crashes, the display pipeline stalls, or the panel/connector is disrupted. You may still hear alerts, feel haptics, or see the device recognized by a computer. Correctly identifying the symptom determines whether a force restart, charge cycle, or software recovery is the right next move.

### Quick Fixes: How to Fix Black Screen on iPhone
- Force restart:
  - iPhone 8 or later: Press Volume Up, then Volume Down, then hold Side until the Apple logo appears.
  - iPhone 7/7 Plus: Hold Volume Down + Side until the Apple logo shows.
  - iPhone 6s or earlier: Hold Home + Top/Side until the Apple logo.
- Charge for 30–60 minutes with an MFi-certified cable and a known-good adapter. Low battery or cold-soaked cells can mimic a black screen.
- Inspect for drops or recent repairs; a partially seated display connector can intermittently blank the screen.
- Connect to a computer (Finder on macOS, iTunes on Windows). If recognized, back up immediately.

### Advanced Software Paths
- Update (non-destructive): Connect to Finder/iTunes, choose Update to reinstall iOS without erasing data.
- Restore (destructive): If Update fails, Restore may be required. Ensure a backup first.
- Recovery/DFU mode: Use when the device won’t boot normally. Proceed cautiously and only if you understand the implications.

### Common Causes and Signals
- Software crash: The compositor stalls; force restart usually resolves this.
- Battery/thermal state: Extremes can temporarily disable the display pipeline.
- Connector or panel issue: Common after impacts or repairs; intermittent black screens are a clue.
- Rare logic board faults: Require professional diagnosis.

### Safety and Data Priorities
- If the device is recognized by a computer, back up first—before deep recovery steps.
- Avoid rapid-fire hard reboots; allow about a minute between attempts.
- Use reputable chargers to prevent brownouts that complicate diagnosis.

### Tips and Pitfalls
- Verify activity: A truly off screen differs from a live-but-black panel. If possible, test with an external display.
- Brief boot flickers suggest video signaling is alive—focus on connectors or iOS reinstall.
- After recovery, watch for recurrence; repeating black screens often mean hardware service is needed.

If force restarts fail, the device isn’t recognized by a computer, or the screen stays black after an iOS reinstall, book a hardware check. Note drops, water exposure, recent repairs, and whether you see faint images under strong light (backlight issues).
